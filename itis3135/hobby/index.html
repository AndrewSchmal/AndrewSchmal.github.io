Grocery Shopping Without a List

One of the first steps to becoming a great home cook is mastering your grocery shopping habits. Instead of following recipes to the letter and buying specialized ingredients you may only use once, I recommend focusing on meal building blocks. This way, you can create flexible meals with the ingredients you have, reducing food waste and unnecessary costs.

Meal Building Blocks

Below are some essential building blocks you can always have on hand. Use them to create a wide variety of dishes, tailored to what you enjoy.

Proteins (Choose 1-2)

  • Chicken Breast / Thighs
  • Ground Meat (Beef, Chicken, Pork, Lamb, Plant-based)
  • Steak or Chops (Beef, Pork, Lamb)
  • Braising Cuts (Pork Shoulder, Beef Chuck Roast, Short Ribs)
  • Beans / Tofu
  • Eggs

Starch Bases (Choose 2-3)

  • Rice
  • Pasta / Noodles
  • Tortillas / Wraps
  • Bread
  • Lettuce / Leafy Greens
  • Tostadas / Chips

Vegetables (Choose 2-3)

  • Carrots
  • Potatoes / Sweet Potatoes
  • Broccoli / Bok Choy
  • Zucchini / Squash
  • Bell Peppers / Poblanos
  • Peas (Frozen)

Meal Flavor Adjusters

  • Mayo / Greek Yogurt / Sour Cream
  • Harissa Paste / Gochujang / Chili Crisp
  • Tomato Paste / Hot Sauce / Sriracha
  • Hummus / Salad Dressing
  • Soy Sauce / Fish Sauce / Oyster Sauce

Mastering Meal Prep

Meal prep is the art of preparing meals or individual ingredients ahead of time, making it easier to enjoy healthy and efficient meals during your busy days. Below are different ways to meal prep, so you can pick what fits your lifestyle the best!

Choose one specific day of the week to do all your meal prep for extra efficiency. Most people choose Saturday or Sunday so they don't have to cook during a weeknight and can prepare all their meals for the month, but do whatever works best for you. I personally prefer to do reheat meals, freezing extras so I don't have to worry about freshness by the time I get to them.
