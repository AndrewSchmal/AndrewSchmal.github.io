Peer Review 1: Cabahug, Aleeza

Evaluation

  • Page leads to the correct location.
  • No spaces or uppercase letters in file names.
  • Uses CRAP principles effectively (Contrast, Repetition, Alignment, Proximity).
    - Alignment could use slight adjustments in tagline spacing.
    - Make sure that the bullet points are indented alongside the list elements, as your bullet points are left-oriented and the words are alone in the middle of the page.
  • Has header, main, footer, and optional nav as required.
  • Header includes the site's branding and uses proper hierarchy.
  • Tagline is visible and consistent on every page.
  • Footer includes validation links and user page menu.
  • Specific project requirements are addressed.
    - I think your project requirements are addressed pretty well. If you wanted, you could consider creating the visualizations of your site map in a software like Lucidchart.

Additional Feedback

  • Strength: Very girlypop/colorful design, stands out compared to most other websites. Navigation works and makes the website look full of stuff to click around in. Styling is consistent and all that too.
  • Improvement: Improve contrast between the navigation text and background for better readability.
  • Suggestion: Make sure the bright pink is readable/accessible, it could be hard to read if it's "an old lady on an ipad looking at the website outside in the sun" to use the example from class. Maybe add hover effects on navigation links and ensure responsiveness for mobile devices (testing viewport and all that stuff).
